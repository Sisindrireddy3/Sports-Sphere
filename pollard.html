MAJOR LEAGUE CRICKET 2024

Rashid, Pooran and Pollard
step up to fire MINY to the
playoffs

by Sports Sphere • Published on Mon, Jul 22, 2024, 10:00 AM

Pollard's cameo was instrumental in MINY sealing qualification © Sportzpics

MI New York's big players stepped up in a must-win clash to fire the defending
champions to the MLC 2024 playoffs. Rashid Khan claimed 3 for 22 as LAKR were
restricted to just 130. MINY then needed to chase that score down in 18.2 overs
to surpass their opposition on Net Run Rate and Nicholas Pooran (35 off 28) and
Kieron Pollard (33* off 12) played key knocks to ensure the target was hauled
down with 18 balls to spare.

MINY seemed up for it from the get go once they elected to bowl on a sluggish
wicket. Trent Boult dismissed Sunil Narine in his first over and although
Jason Roy got off to a brisk start with a six off Boult and a pair of boundaries
off Nosthush Kenjige, MINY also bagged the wicket of Unmukt Chand in a 45-run
PowerPlay. The dismissal of the former India U-19 captain going into the
middle-overs phase was particularly critical given how spin was expected to
play a big part.

Rashid struck with just his fourth delivery as a googly snuck under Roy's attempted
slog and knocked back the offstump. At the other end, Kenjige had David Miller
holing out to long on to an attempted heave, leaving the Knight Riders struggling
at 62 for 4 at the half-way point of their innings. Between them, the two MINY
spinners took 5 for 44 in eight overs to send the LAKR innings into a tailspin.
Kenjige then dismissed Saif Badar while Rashid castled a rusty Nitish Kumar and
then trapped Spencer Johnson LBW after a missed sweep.

At 101 for 9, LAKR were headed to a grossly sub-par score but they were propped
up to something of a fighting total by an Andre Russell (35 off 21) cameo. The
West Indian struck two back-to-back boundaries off Rashid and then repeated that
feat in the following over bowled by Romario Shepherd. Farming the strike with
only No.11 Joshua Little for company, Russell took down Boult for two fours and
a six. By the 19th over, Russell had powered LAKR to 130 but his attempts to add
further to the toal in the final over was put paid by a clever short ball from
Kieron Pollard that rushed Russell on the pull and ballooned up off the spice.

Brief scores: LA Knight Riders 130 in 19.1 overs (Andre Russell 35; Rashid Khan
3-22, Nosthush Kenjige 2-22) lost to MI New York 134/6 in 17 overs (Nicholas Pooran
35, Kieron Pollard 33*; Sunil Narine 2-24) by four wickets
